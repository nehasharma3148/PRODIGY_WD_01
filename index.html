IRON MAN

Iron Man is a Marvel superhero who wears a high-tech suit of armor that grants him incredible abilities and weapons.His real name is Tony Stark, a genius billionaire inventor and philanthropist who runs Stark Industries, a leading defense contractor.

THOR

Thor Odinson is a fictional character portrayed by Chris Hemsworth in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) media franchise1. He is based on the Marvel Comics character of the same name and the Norse mythological god of the same name. Thor is the son of Odin, the Asgardian prince, and wields the mystical mallet Mjolnir as he protects innocents across the realms on his own and as a member of the Avengers.

HULK

The Hulk is a fictional superhero created by Marvel Comics. He is the alter ego of scientist Bruce Banner, who transforms into a green-skinned, muscular giant when he is angry or stressed. The Hulk's strength and durability increase with his emotions, making him one of the most powerful beings in the Marvel universe1. The Hulk was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby in 1962.

Avengers Assemble is an American animated television series based on the fictional Marvel Comics superhero team known as the Avengers. Designed to capitalize on the success of the 2012 film The Avengers, the series premiered on Disney XD on May 26, 2013,[1][3] as the successor to The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes. It previously aired alongside Ultimate Spider-Man and Guardians of the Galaxy as a part of the Marvel Universe block. Joe Casey, Joe Kelly, Duncan Rouleau and Steven T. Seagle, known collectively as Man of Action, developed the series and were executive producers on seasons 1 and 2.
